CHILDREN’S PRIVACY - SPECIAL NOTE TO PARENTS

We value your children’s privacy.

It is important that you understand that we do use social networks in our game to allow players who are 13 years old or older to have fun by exchanging their gameplay events with other players over the age of 13.  The Social Networks we use are based on prominent third party social networks, like Facebook, Apple’s Game Center, Google+, and Amazon’s GameCircle. We rely on the age restrictions from these networks to make sure only players over the age of 13 can share information.  It is up to you to make sure your child is of the required age to be using a social network.

We do allow players who are 13 years old or older to participate in the Forums on our Sites and in surveys to share their experiences of playing the game.

With the exception of information collected from Social Networks, Forums, and Surveys, we do not collect any personal identifying information in game, including names or email addresses, from players, except when we believe a player’s safety is at risk.

The Internet and connected applications offers a world of opportunity for children. Your guidance and involvement are essential to help ensure that children have a safe and rewarding online experience. We encourage you to visit the http://getnetwise.org site and look through the "Kids Safety" section together. Your efforts to instill responsible information practices will help steer your children to age-appropriate sites and will go a long way toward ensuring that your children have enriching experiences online.

YOUR VOICE

We have a feature called “Your Voice” in some of our games that asks users questions about pop culture, current events, and other interesting issues. Users can respond with one out of four multiple choice answers.  There is also the option to skip a question. These answers are shared with friends within a user’s Social Networks. These answers are also aggregated so that users can see what users, in aggregate, choose as the most popular answers.  The answers are purposefully multiple choice answers only, so that they do not reveal any personally identifiable information.

COOKIES

When you visit our Services, we may use cookies and similar technologies like pixels, Software Development Kits (SDKs) and web beacons, to collect this anonymous information for the purpose of the internal operations of our Services. A "cookie" is a piece of information that a web site or an app transfers to a user's device for record-keeping purposes. As a user moves from one page or one screen to another, a cookie is used to tell us that it is the same device. We also may use a cookie to keep track of your selections. You may set your device to accept or not accept cookies and you may delete any cookies that you have received. However, should you decide to disable any cookies we place on your device, you may not be able to use certain services or features on our Platforms. A "web beacon" is a one-pixel image (which may be visible or transparent), which may be embedded on a web page or e-mail message, and which may also provide us with information about usage of that page or e-mail message.
